
# Rome 3-Day Family Plan
**Group:** 6 Adults + 4 Children  
**Dates:** June 7–10

---

## Overview
This optimized plan highlights:
- What needs tickets
- What is FREE
- When to book in advance
- What can be done casually

---

## Day 1 — Ancient Rome (June 7)

### Morning: Colosseum Area
- **Ticket Required:**
    - Colosseum
    - Roman Forum
    - Palatine Hill
    - _Usually one combined ticket covers all three._
- **Must Buy in Advance:**
    - Especially for 10 people
    - **Recommended:**
        - Guided tour
        - Morning slot (8:30–10 AM)
- **Time needed:** 3–4 hours total

### Lunch Area
- **FREE to walk around:**
    - Via dei Fori Imperiali
    - Piazza Venezia (exterior)

### Afternoon: Historic Rome Walking Tour
- **FREE:**
    - Trevi Fountain  
        _No ticket. Go in the evening for beautiful lighting._
    - Spanish Steps
    - Pantheon (outside)
        - _Pantheon now requires paid entry for inside, but outside piazza area is free._
        - _For your group: Outside only is enough unless deeply interested in history._

---

## Day 2 — Vatican Day (June 8)

### Morning: Vatican Museums
- **Ticket Required:**
    - Vatican Museums
    - Sistine Chapel
- **Must Buy in Advance:**
    - This is one of the most crowded places in Europe
    - **Recommended:**
        - Skip-the-line guided tour
        - 8–9 AM entry
- **Time needed:** 4–5 hours

### Afternoon: St. Peter’s Basilica
- **FREE ENTRY:**
    - St. Peter’s Basilica
        - _You only pay if:_
            - Dome climb
            - Guided access
        - _Security line exists but no entry ticket needed._
        - _This is one of the best FREE attractions in the world._

### Evening
- **FREE:**
    - Castel Sant’Angelo (exterior area and bridge)
        - _Inside museum requires ticket._
        - _For your family: Exterior + bridge photos are enough._

---

## Day 3 — Relaxed Rome (June 9)

### Morning
- **FREE:**
    - Villa Borghese
        - _Excellent for children and relaxing._

### Optional Paid Activity
- **Ticket Required:**
    - Galleria Borghese
        - _Very hard to get tickets._
        - _Skip for this trip unless your family loves art museums._

### Afternoon
- **FREE:**
    - Trastevere
        - _Best area for:_
            - Food
            - Walking
            - Atmosphere
            - Family dinner
    - Santa Maria in Trastevere
        - _Beautiful church. No ticket required._

## My Recommendation for Your Family

For 10 people:
- **Spend money only on:**
    - Colosseum
    - Vatican Museums
- **Keep everything else relaxed and free**

Rome itself is the attraction.

You can spend entire evenings simply walking around:
- Trevi Fountain
- Piazza Navona
- Trastevere
- Vatican surroundings

…and have an amazing experience without additional tickets.
